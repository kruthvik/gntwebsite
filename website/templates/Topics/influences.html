Picasso Influences

                Pablo Picasso is often regarded as one of the most talented and influential artists of all time. But, to get this far, Picasso took inspiration from many artists. His artwork is so mysterious, it is hard to find his inspirations and influences. Picasso’s short Blue Period lasted for 3 years from 1901 to 1904 where Picasso only painted with blue and green tones. This was caused by his depression when his friend committed suicide. Picasso’s “African Period” lasted from 1906 to 1909. During this period, Picasso was inspired by African art styles. This was the period where Picasso invented cubism. From WW1 to the 1930s, Picasso made art inspired by Italy and its history. During this period, Picasso’s art has also become somewhat more political. Picasso was also influenced by many painters. For example, Les Demoiselles d'Avignon was deeply inspired by African art, as well as Paul Cezanne for the brushwork of the art. The obscure painting, “Large Nude in A Red Armchair” made in the 1920s by Picasso has possibly been inspired by Salvador Dali and Joan Miro.
