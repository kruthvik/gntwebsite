Painting Techniques

         Pablo Picasso is truly an amazing and creative artist. But he invented special techniques and styles in order to paint amazingly. One of Picasso’s most renowned painting methods is the “sugar-lift” method. This allowed to paint freely on a metal plate directly. It was used in many of Picasso’s painting. This method also allows more creativity and abstract art in the artwork. Picasso also used certain printmaking techniques sometimes with the sugar-lift method. There are many of them, but Intaglio is one Picasso used more often. In this technique, lines of paper are printed onto the original paper made of acids. Then, a metal plate is wiped to paste the ink onto it. This method allowed to recreate paintings. He also drew a sketch / outline. He shaded and continued onto this with gentle brush strokes. Like the sugar-lift method, this was used in his abstract paintings.
